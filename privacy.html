Photography and Video Policy

A Scout is to be trusted, A scout is friendly and considerate, a Scout has self-respect and respect for other. (extracts from the Scout Law)

Applies to Members, parents, leaders, visitors (anyone present at a Scout event)

Where At all events organised by the Section or Group

When Application at all Scout events

Purpose The Beavers, Cubs, Scouts and Explorers will be succeeding at new challenges and learning new skills. It is appropriate to record some events in pictures or video. The sections and group also wish to be able to show others what we do, by showing pictures and video to parents, displays in the church hall and (limited) displays on the website. This helps parents to understand what we do and lets prospective members get an idea of what they could join in to do.

The following principles will be followed by members, parents, leaders and others:

Location Pictures and video will only be taken in "public". Pictures should not be taken in accommodation areas or other private spaces. In summary each member is entitled to privacy.

Situation Pictures will generally record the activities of the group. Each of us have times when we are emotional or upset, in such a situation, privacy should be respectected.

It is expected that a few pictures will be taken to record the best of an event. It is not necessary or appropiate to take large numbers of pictures.

Publication The Scout Group and Explorer Unit use pictures in the following ways: Show at parents' evenings Display in small numbers in the church halls Display in small numbers on the website In public displays, pictures are of activity in groups and members are not named Appropriate pictures will be shared in the closed facebook group for parents.

The following principles should be respected by all members, parents and leaders:

In general pictures will not be published (except as above) and names will not be published. Any member, parent or leader taking pictures at a Scout event should expect to share the pictures taken with leaders, members and parents (eg using the closed facebook group).

Sanction

Leaders - breaches of these principals would be a matter for the Group Scout Leader, or District Commissioner. Members or parents - breaches would be a matter for the Group Scout Leader. Any breaches should be rectified, and if appropriate either suspension or expulsion of the leader or member may be appropriate. We hope that parents will find this policy to be helpful, appropriate and reassuring. We take our child protection responsibilities seriously.
